Online Furniture Market in India

FEB 01, 2021
By - Seventh Square

According to RedSeer Consulting, the online furniture market in India grew at a CAGR of approximately 80-85% to reach approximately $310 million in FY19 as compared to $104 million in FY17, and is likely to have touched $700 million in FY20. But despite this rapid growth, online has less than 3% share in the overall furniture market which is pegged at $17 billion.

The pandemic, over the last few months, has pushed forward some upcoming trends in the massive and growing Indian Furniture market.

Organized Retail

Once dominated by mom & pop stores, the furniture market has witnessed major shift towards organized retail and e-commerce. As supply chains become more efficient, the cost for e-tailing and building a multi-channel furniture brand has come down substantially. Consumer preferences in metros and tier-I have also pushed shift to organized retail, with smaller brands emerging across the country.

Consumer Preferences

As lockdown forced consumers to stay inside their homes, the need for comfortable and multi-functional furniture increased. This was duly serviced by e-commerce players and organized segment, including the new major entrant IKEA. Consumers, having plenty of time in hand during lockdown, learnt more about online shopping and many became comfortable with over time, which increased the demand for suitable online portals providing them a convenient and cost effective way to buy their furniture.
As consumers educated themselves over lockdown, return rates have also fallen across categories which has helped many sellers become even more confident about selling online.

Supply Chains

As supply chains also strengthened, processing orders safely and efficiently became easy, giving a boost to both sellers as well as consumers, who started trusting online more than offline retail. With current infrastructure, it became possible for a seller in Mumbai to sell to a consumer in a small town in Himachal, which otherwise wouldn’t have been possible. Consumers and sellers see this infra upgrade as a win-win where sellers, who have developed a good portfolio can showcase their products to every part of the country and the consumer can get a product which he wouldn’t find in his own town.

With continuous improvement in tech and logistics, e-commerce in furniture will continue to grow as consumers in tier-IV also start trusting the process. Multi-functional furniture will gain more traction as consumers look to save space and build their home better. Services will also play an important part in e-commerce as consumer focuses more on experience.
