Construction Steel market in India and Emerging trends

FEB 01, 2021
By - Seventh Square

Construction Steel includes Structural Steel, Rebars, Sheets, Pipes, etc. which are used in Building & Construction. It is a huge market and accounts for about 40% of the country's total steel demand. In this 40%, the housing sector alone comprises of upto 75% demand, with the remainder being filled by industrial & commercial. As a developing country, India’s per capita consumption of steel is also among the lowest in the world and there’s room for a lot of growth.

Demand & Growth

The Construction Steel market was growing at a mere 3% before the pandemic and declined more than 10% in the pandemic. Up until now, the market paints a grim picture, however, future predictions are much better. Building Steel will register around 10-25% rebound in FY22 and continue to grow at 5-6% for five years, which is much better than before the pandemic.

Several demand drivers are helping the B&C Steel market to grow, such as:

  • Affordable Housing Push under PMAY-U & PMAY-R
  • Real Estate Construction. ~1.2 Billion sq. ft. under construction in the country in FY21 as per a report by Crisil.
  • Rapid Urbanisation from rural areas to urban centres. India expected to be 40% urban by 2030.
  • Change from Kutcha houses in rural areas to proper housing
  • Nuclearisation of families. Average family size continues to fall, which leads to more and more housing demand.
  • Rising Middle & Rich Class of India, who go on to buy second & even third houses.

These factors are pushing the B&C steel market alone from 3% growth in FY19 to more than 6% projected in FY22.

Emerging Trends

As we continue to grow as a nation, changes in building structure can also be seen.
More & more developers & individuals are moving towards steel-based structures instead of RCC.
Instead of using clay tiles, etc. for roofing, people in rural and poor areas are now using Metal Roofing Sheets, mostly steel. Pre-Engineered Buildings (PEB) are emerging as a strong trend in Industrial Real Estate as RCC Factories & Warehouses are fading out. Commercial use of PEB is still nascent and growing.

Digitisation

Steel Sector is one of the last to adopt digitisation in the country, but can benefit a lot from it. As per a report by PWC, digitised value chain can improve EBITDA by 2-4% for steel making companies and reduce inventory by 10 or more days.

A varied client base & complicated distribution channels with various margin implications are major hurdles for the steel industry. This can be solved by more focus on D2C channels using companies’ already built storage & logistics network in the country. This will help in gaining direct access to customers & customer data as well as control & maintain the price levels to the consumers. Implementation of various tech like IOT (Internet of things) can help measurably in this aspect.
