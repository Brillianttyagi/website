Green Buildings & India : Build back Better

FEB 01, 2021
By - Seventh Square

Image: Turning Torso, Malmö, Sweden (Green Skyscraper)

The Construction industry in India is one of the fastest growing now at >9% CAGR and contributes ~10% to our GDP. Unfortunately, this will lead to a significant increase in carbon emissions unless we move towards 'Green Buildings' , which currently are at only 5% of existing development.

What is a Green Building?

Green or Sustainable Buildings make the Building resource efficient and reduce construction & operating emissions. They can be identified using Green Building Rating systems which evaluate the Building on several Parameters like:

  • Location & Transportation : The location of the project and available public transportation facilities in the area help in reducing pollution and efficient commuting for residents.
  • Material & Resources : Using Green or Recycled materials help in reducing waste and emissions in the environment.
  • Water Efficiency : Focus on water has increased as major metros are running out of it. Minimal use of potable water and recycling within the premises also help in a major way in making the environment better and sustainable.
  • Energy & Atmosphere: Promoting efficient energy use and providing a sustainable & quality environment inside the project provides lower energy usage & costs as well as better lifestyle.
  • Sustainable Sites: Making the project in such a way that it utilises natural resources as much as possible such as accessible sunlight inside the building, solar powered project, water harvesting, etc. help in reducing energy & resource consumption from polluting & scarce resources.

  • Innovation : Innovative Buildings which improve habitability and provide sustainable solutions are a major factor too.

Ratings systems like LEED (Leadership in Energy & Environmental Design) is the most widely used green building rating system in the world. India itself has GRIHA (Green Rating for integrated habitat assessment) for rating Green Buildings on a three-tier model.

Case for Green Buildings

  • Although initial construction & design costs are higher, the amount can be recovered over and above in a short span of 3-7 years. Currently in India, MNCs and Luxury housing are the major investors in Green Buildings as there are other factors considered beyond cost.
  • Also, after the pandemic, people have started moving towards a healthier lifestyle and environment which may make a strong case for Green Buildings in the future.
